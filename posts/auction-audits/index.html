Profitable Cheating is Detectable

statsmath

May 18, 2026

Any profitable deviation from an auction's honest law leaves auditable evidence

[NOTE]: This is largely a scratchpad for some notes/thoughts on auctions that'll hopefully turn into something cool later.

Ad auctions run thousands of rounds per second, and an auctioneer who subtly inflates prices or skews allocations can make a ton of money over time. A natural question is whether this is detectable; not asympotically, but sequentially, wiht a controlled false-alarm rate at every round.

The answer is yes, and proving it is quite simple: the core ideas is that gain requires changing the law of the transcript and the likelihood ratio is a martingale which generates evidence.

Transcript and Laws

Let (Xt)(X_t)(Xt​) be the full auditable transcript at round ttt: bids, scores, reserves, allocations, prices, clicks, randomness seeds; everything the auditor can observe. Let Ft−1\mathcal{F}_{t-1}Ft−1​ be the filtration generated by all prior rounds.

We model cheating as a change in the distribution of observed outcomes. Define two conditional laws:

  • Pt(⋅∣Ft−1)P_t(\cdot \mid \mathcal{F}_{t-1})Pt​(⋅∣Ft−1​): the honest law of XtX_tXt​, i.e. what the auction is supposed to produce.
  • Qt(⋅∣Ft−1)Q_t(\cdot \mid \mathcal{F}_{t-1})Qt​(⋅∣Ft−1​): the law induced by a deviating strategy.

This makes no commitment about the mechanism of cheating, only that a cheating strategy must produce transcripts drawn from some law other than PtP_tPt​.

Evidence Martingale

Define the per-round log-likelihood ratio and its running sum:

ℓt≐log⁡qt(Xt∣Ft−1)pt(Xt∣Ft−1),Et≐∑s=1tℓs,Lt≐eEt. \ell_t \doteq \log \frac{q_t(X_t \mid \mathcal{F}_{t-1})}{p_t(X_t \mid \mathcal{F}_{t-1})}, \qquad E_t \doteq \sum_{s=1}^t \ell_s, \qquad L_t \doteq e^{E_t}. ℓt​≐logpt​(Xt​∣Ft−1​)qt​(Xt​∣Ft−1​)​,Et​≐s=1∑t​ℓs​,Lt​≐eEt​.

Under the honest law PPP, the ratio Lt/Lt−1=qt(Xt)/pt(Xt)L_t / L_{t-1} = q_t(X_t)/p_t(X_t)Lt​/Lt−1​=qt​(Xt​)/pt​(Xt​) has conditional expectation

EP ⁣[LtLt−1 ∣ Ft−1]=∫qt(x)pt(x) pt(x) dx=∫qt(x) dx=1, \mathbb{E}_P\!\left[\frac{L_t}{L_{t-1}} \,\Big|\, \mathcal{F}_{t-1}\right] = \int \frac{q_t(x)}{p_t(x)} \, p_t(x) \, dx = \int q_t(x) \, dx = 1, EP​[Lt−1​Lt​​​Ft−1​]=∫pt​(x)qt​(x)​pt​(x)dx=∫qt​(x)dx=1,

so (Lt)(L_t)(Lt​) is a nonneg PPP-martingale with L0=1L_0 = 1L0​=1. This is the no false evidence under honest play condition: the evidence process has no drift when the auction runs honestly.

Under the cheating law QQQ, the same log-increment satisfies

EQ[ℓt∣Ft−1]=DKL(Qt∥Pt)≥0, \mathbb{E}_Q[\ell_t \mid \mathcal{F}_{t-1}] = D_{\mathrm{KL}}(Q_t \| P_t) \ge 0, EQ​[ℓt​∣Ft−1​]=DKL​(Qt​∥Pt​)≥0,

so (Et)(E_t)(Et​) is a QQQ-submartingale with predictable drift equal to the conditional KL divergence. The same process is a martingale under honesty and accumulates upward drift under cheating.

Gain Implies KL Drift

The hard part is connecting the evidence process to actual profit. Let gtg_tgt​ be a one-round gain observable -- the extra profit extracted relative to honest play. Define the conditional excess gain:

Δt≐EQ[gt∣Ft−1]−EP[gt∣Ft−1]. \Delta_t \doteq \mathbb{E}_Q[g_t \mid \mathcal{F}_{t-1}] - \mathbb{E}_P[g_t \mid \mathcal{F}_{t-1}]. Δt​≐EQ​[gt​∣Ft−1​]−EP​[gt​∣Ft−1​].

To close the argument we need: positive Δt\Delta_tΔt​ forces positive DKLD_{\mathrm{KL}}DKL​. There are two natural versions.

Pinsker Bound

If 0≤gt≤Bt0 \le g_t \le B_t0≤gt​≤Bt​, then by Pinsker's inequality,

Δt≤Bt⋅TV(Qt,Pt)≤Bt12DKL(Qt∥Pt), \Delta_t \le B_t \cdot \mathrm{TV}(Q_t, P_t) \le B_t \sqrt{\tfrac{1}{2} D_{\mathrm{KL}}(Q_t \| P_t)}, Δt​≤Bt​⋅TV(Qt​,Pt​)≤Bt​21​DKL​(Qt​∥Pt​)​,

which rearranges to:

KL Lower Bound (Pinsker)
DKL(Qt∥Pt)≥2(ΔtBt)2. D_{\mathrm{KL}}(Q_t \| P_t) \ge 2\left(\frac{\Delta_t}{B_t}\right)^2. DKL​(Qt​∥Pt​)≥2(Bt​Δt​​)2.

OT Bound

If gtg_tgt​ is KtK_tKt​-Lipschitz in a transcript metric dtd_tdt​, then by the Kantorovich dual,

Δt≤Kt W1(Qt,Pt). \Delta_t \le K_t \, W_1(Q_t, P_t). Δt​≤Kt​W1​(Qt​,Pt​).

If the honest law satisfies a transport-entropy inequality W1(Qt,Pt)2≤2CtDKL(Qt∥Pt)W_1(Q_t, P_t)^2 \le 2C_t D_{\mathrm{KL}}(Q_t \| P_t)W1​(Qt​,Pt​)2≤2Ct​DKL​(Qt​∥Pt​),

DKL(Qt∥Pt)≥Δt22CtKt2. D_{\mathrm{KL}}(Q_t \| P_t) \ge \frac{\Delta_t^2}{2 C_t K_t^2}. DKL​(Qt​∥Pt​)≥2Ct​Kt2​Δt2​​.

The OT version is cleaner for ad auctions, where transcript coordinates have a natural Lipschitz structure and the honest law often satisfies a log-Sobolev or Talagrand inequality.

Evidence Accumulation and Audit Tests

Plugging either bound back into the drift of EtE_tEt​ and summing:

EQ[ET]=∑t=1TEQ[ℓt]≥∑t=1TΔt22CtKt2. \mathbb{E}_Q[E_T] = \sum_{t=1}^T \mathbb{E}_Q[\ell_t] \ge \sum_{t=1}^T \frac{\Delta_t^2}{2 C_t K_t^2}. EQ​[ET​]=t=1∑T​EQ​[ℓt​]≥t=1∑T​2Ct​Kt2​Δt2​​.

The right-hand side is a sum of squared excess gains. As long as someone keeps cheating and extracting edge, ETE_TET​ grows without bound.

Since (Lt)(L_t)(Lt​) is a nonneg PPP-martingale with L0=1L_0 = 1L0​=1, Ville's inequality gives1

P ⁣(sup⁡tLt≥1α)≤α, P\!\left(\sup_t L_t \ge \frac{1}{\alpha}\right) \le \alpha, P(tsup​Lt​≥α1​)≤α,

equivalently P(sup⁡tEt≥log⁡(1/α))≤αP(\sup_t E_t \ge \log(1/\alpha)) \le \alphaP(supt​Et​≥log(1/α))≤α. We define the stopping time

τα≐inf⁡{t:Et≥log⁡(1/α)}. \tau_\alpha \doteq \inf\{t : E_t \ge \log(1/\alpha)\}. τα​≐inf{t:Et​≥log(1/α)}.

Anytime-Valid Audit Test
The test "flag at τα\tau_\alphaτα​" satisfies:
  • False alarm control: under honest play, P(τα<∞)≤αP(\tau_\alpha < \infty) \leq \alphaP(τα​<∞)≤α, simultaneously over all stopping times.
  • Power: under sustained cheating, EtE_tEt​ has positive drift, so τα<∞\tau_\alpha < \inftyτα​<∞ a.s. whenever cumulative excess gain is unbounded.

The guarantee is anytime-valid: the false alarm bound holds at every round, not just at a pre-committed horizon.2
